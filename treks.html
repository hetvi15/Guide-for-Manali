MESMERIZING MANALI

HEAVEN AND BEYOND

Because the best views comes after the highest climbs!!

One of the most stellar destinations that offer unbeatable trekking trails for the adventurous souls and nature lovers, Himachal Pradesh is truly a heaven for trekking enthusiasts. As they say, “the wealth of nature is best savored when touched and sensed”, one has to experience the thrill, embrace the challenge, enjoy the air and behold the view to soak in the soul of this beautiful bounty of nature.

1. Beas-Kund trek

Also known as Vyasa Kund trek, the base point of the trek is the Solang valley, located at a distance of 15 kms from Manali. Dotted with vast grasslands, small rivulets, coniferous forests, and scenic campsites, Beas Kund trek treats you to the panoramic views of Deo Tibba, Hanuman Tibba, Dhundhi, Indrasen Peak and Pir Panjal range. The easy to moderate trekking trail of the trek makes it a big hit among amateurs as well as the beginners. The best part of the trek is the Beas Kund lake located at the summit of the trek

2. Bhrigu lake trek

A perfect 3-day weekend escapade that takes you to the staggering altitude of 14,000 feet in such a short span, Bhrigu Lake Trek attracts beginners and seasoned trekkers alike. The approximate trekking distance of 25 km traverses through beautiful Alpine regions, green-carpeted meadows, splendid campsites, quaint mountain villages and floors you with the view of colossal peaks of Hanuman Tibba .

3. Hampta pass trek

Hampta Pass is the most dramatic crossover trek in the Pir Panjal Range of the Himalayas. With a trekking distance of 28 kms, this 5-day trekking extravaganza takes you to an ambitious altitude of 14,010 feet. The easy to moderate trek captivates you with the most contrasting landscapes that range from vertical rock-walls to expansive grasslands, effervescent streams, silver waterfalls to hanging glaciers to the picturesque campsites and the barren and cold deserts of Lahaul and Spiti.

4. Pin Parvati trek

If you are looking for 11 days of sheer exhilaration, then Pin Parvati trek is your true calling! The imposing altitude of 17,457 feet of Pin Parvati Pass leaves less for imagination. The 112 km difficult level trek is peppered with a shifting panorama of lush prairie grasslands on one end a nd a cold barren desert of Spiti region on the other end of the spectrum. Colossal pastures, Buddhist hamlets, freezing glaciers, Hot Springs of Parvati Kund and an assortment of vivid culture-the challenging Pin Parvati trek is certainly not for the feeble-hearted.

5. Chanderkhani Pass

A short yet beautiful trek of 3 days which traverses a distance of 21 kms, Chanderkhani Pass trek brings you to an altitude of 12,000 feet. Nestled amidst the best of Himalayan peaks, the trekking trail journeys along the mammoth Barabangal, Pir Panjal and Parvati Ranges and offers the most splendid view of the Deo Tibba and Hanuman Tibba peaks. The quaint mountain villages of Naggar and Rumsu and Chandrakhani Pass are the star attractions of this trek which is covered in the snow till the end of May.
